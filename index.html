Project Symbiosis

Our Vision The Journey The Lab

Project Symbiosis started with a simple but powerful goal: to bridge the gap between artificial intelligence and human empathy. We realized that in healthcare, there is a silent struggle for patients who can’t speak for themselves—children, elderly people with dementia, or those with disabilities who can’t tell a doctor where it hurts. Our idea was to create a Pain Expression Decoder that uses a camera to read those subtle facial cues and muscle movements that the human eye might miss, turning them into an objective pain score that healthcare providers can actually use.

This project wouldn’t be possible without our diverse crew. We have Mohammad Shabeer Noorzad and Ananya Relia handling the deep dives into medical research and academic writing, while Ukhun Majesty Osezele keeps our communication sharp. On the technical side, Noor Hanafi, Navya Kolati, and Mahado Abdirahman are the ones bringing the code to life, working with Python and web development to make sure our "biological translator" actually functions in a real-world clinical setting.
